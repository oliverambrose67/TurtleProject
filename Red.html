Endangered Turtles of Alabama

Alabama Red-bellied Turtle

(Pseudemys alabamensis)

Photo: Outdoor Alabama

About the Species

A close look at the distinctive head stripes.

The Alabama Red-bellied Turtle is a large, freshwater turtle and is designated as Alabama's state reptile. It was listed as endangered in 1987 due to a small and declining population.

Key Features:

  • Carapace (Top Shell): Dark, olive-green to black with reddish or yellowish vertical markings on the sides.
  • Plastron (Bottom Shell): Typically reddish or orange, giving the turtle its name.
  • Head: Marked with prominent yellow or light-colored stripes.
  • Size: Females are larger, with shells reaching up to 15 inches in length.

Habitat & Distribution

The Mobile-Tensaw River Delta, primary habitat for the turtle.

This turtle has one of the smallest ranges of any turtle in North America.

Habitat: They prefer freshwater rivers, streams, and reservoirs with soft bottoms, abundant aquatic vegetation for food, and plenty of logs or snags for basking in the sun.

Location in Alabama: They are found almost exclusively in the fresh and brackish waters of the Mobile-Tensaw River Delta in Mobile and Baldwin counties. Their range is mostly confined to the lower parts of the Mobile and Tensaw rivers.

Nesting & Locomotion

Example of turtle tracks leading from a nesting site.

Diagram of typical turtle footprints, showing alternating limb movement.

Nesting Habits:

  • Nesting occurs from May through July.
  • Females leave the water to find open, sandy, and well-drained areas exposed to direct sunlight.
  • They dig a nest with their hind feet and lay a clutch of 4 to 9 eggs.
  • A significant threat is nest predation by animals like fire ants, raccoons, and fish crows.

Locomotion (Movement):

  • In Water: They are powerful swimmers, using their webbed feet for propulsion.
  • On Land: Movement is slow and deliberate. They walk in a "diagonal" pattern, moving opposite limbs (e.g., front right and rear left) at the same time. This creates a distinct track pattern in soft soil or sand.
